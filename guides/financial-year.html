Differences Between the Standard and Indian Financial Year

When carefully tracking your amortization schedule, calculating overall cumulative interest paid, or preparing intensely for income tax season, the specific 12-month window you are looking at is critically important. Different countries and international accounting standards utilize widely different 12-month cycle definitions.

The Standard Calendar Year (January to December)

The Standard Calendar Year is unequivocally the default metric used across most of the world to track daily life, commercial events, and broad financial timelines. It simply runs uninterrupted from January 1st to December 31st.

For most personal finance software and general budgeting tracking, this timeline is highly intuitive. If you explicitly want to know how much total interest you paid in "2025," a standard calculator simply adds up all the interest payments spanning those specific twelve months.

The Indian Financial Year (April to March)

For corporate accounting, mandatory government reporting, and most critically, personal taxation directly in India, the government legally enforces a perfectly specific fiscal cycle known universally as the Indian Financial Year (FY). This cycle distinctly begins on April 1st and officially closes perfectly on March 31st of the subsequent calendar year.

For example, the financial year designated explicitly as "FY 2025-26" begins on April 1, 2025, and ends entirely on March 31, 2026.

  • Section 80C: Properly allows a generous deduction on the home loan *principal* successfully repaid.

To accurately and legally claim these massive deductions perfectly on your Income Tax Return (ITR), you absolutely must reliably know the exact sum of principal and interest you routinely paid restricted entirely between April 1st and March 31st.
